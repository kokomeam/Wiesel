Product Requirements · Marketing Studio · Web + Public

Autonomous Email Campaigns —
from a finished course to a sent, tracked, self-improving launch.

A creator finishes a course and opens Marketing Studio. They pick a goal, choose a permission-based lead list, and the AI reads the syllabus to write a complete 4-email launch sequence — every email editable by card or by chat. A compliance reviewer sweeps it; the creator approves; and WiseSel sends the sequence automatically, on schedule, suppressing anyone who unsubscribes or bounces, while a single event stream tracks opens, clicks, and enrollments. The autonomy is real — but every list-facing send waits for one human “go.”

01 — Purpose & Goals

A course that no one hears about earns nothing. Make the launch itself the product.

WiseSel already turns expertise into a course and — with the Marketing Assistant foundation — into a landing page, a lead list, and an event stream. This milestone builds the piece that actually converts: a course-aware Autonomous Email Marketing Agent that plans a goal-driven campaign, writes a reviewable email sequence from the syllabus, reviews it for compliance, and — once a human approves — sends and follows up on its own, tracking every event and suggesting the next move. Ambitious in the AI; conservative in what reaches a real inbox.

The problem

  • Marketing is a different skill than teaching. Creators finish a course and freeze: no list, no sequence, no idea when to follow up.
  • Email sequencing is hard to write from scratch — and generic AI copy tools don't know what the course teaches, so every email starts from a blank page.
  • Consent and deliverability are confusing and risky. Most creators don't know list-permission rules, unsubscribe requirements, or how to avoid the spam folder — and one careless blast can burn a domain.
  • The tooling is fragmented. An email platform, a spreadsheet of contacts, a copywriter, and an analytics dashboard — none of them course-aware, none of them talking to each other.
  • Creators want automation but not a loose cannon. They'll happily let AI do 90% of the work — but not send unreviewed content to their audience.
Builds on an existing foundation — this is the campaign layer, not a rebuild

The three spines are already shipped and verified (~157 checks): one typed tool layer (executeMarketingTool), one event stream (analytics_event + a subscriber-status reducer), and one reversibility-graded governance gate (marketing_action ledger), plus a 9-table schema, our own scheduler/outbox with idempotent sends, a mock→Resend swap seam, and a reason→act→pause agent loop. This milestone adds the campaign-centric layer on top: a full campaign lifecycle, goal-driven 4-email generation with per-step fields, consent-first lead lists with manual import, a Compliance & Trust reviewer, explicit follow-up rules, sender identity, and the 26-tool Marketing Chat surface. Section 4 marks every capability exists / extend / new.

Goals

Course-grounded, goal-driven campaigns

Pick a goal (launch, re-engage, convert interest, promote an offer); the AI reads the syllabus, outcomes, and tone and writes a complete sequence — not a blank template.

Editable, then trustworthy autonomy

Every email is editable by card or chat; a compliance reviewer runs before launch; and only after explicit approval does WiseSel send, follow up, and suppress on its own.

Permission-first list building

Manual import demands a consent confirmation; interest-list signups capture provenance; unsubscribed and bounced contacts are suppressed forever. Consent is a precondition, not a footnote.

Real tracking, real analytics

Sent, delivered, opened, clicked, bounced, unsubscribed, enrolled — one event stream drives the dashboard and the agent's next suggestion. Missing metrics say “Not available yet,” never a fake number.

A marketing co-pilot with tools

A Cursor-style chat that can plan, rewrite an email, generate variants, adjust timing, run compliance, pause a campaign, and explain performance — every change staged as a reviewable patch.

Built to extend cleanly

The schema and tool surface leave clean seams for social posting, real A/B testing, segmentation, and advanced analytics — added later without reworking the core.

Non-goals (this milestone)

  • No social or forum auto-posting — social/forum/offer copy exists as a draft generator only (copy/export, never auto-publish).
  • No real A/B testing engine — variants are generated for the creator to pick manually; no recipient splitting, no winner declaration, no statistics.
  • No advanced segmentation, no CRM/pipeline, no multi-channel orchestration.
  • No cold outreach to scraped or purchased lists — ever. And no unsupervised campaign creation that reaches a real person without creator approval.
  • No Stripe/checkout changes — “enrollment” is recorded as an event; payment lives elsewhere. Per-creator sending domains are a later seam (MVP sends from the platform's verified domain).

Hard non-negotiables (enforced by design)
  • Approved autonomy, never unsupervised. The AI may generate, plan, and prepare a whole campaign — but sending requires explicit creator approval. Once approved, it operates only within the approved content, list, schedule, and rules.
  • Every send routes the governance gate. Agent, card, or button — there is no second write path; even the mock send passes the gate, and even a test send to the creator's own address is gated.
  • Consent & deliverability are mandatory. Every subscriber carries source + consent provenance; every marketing email carries a compliant footer and a working one-click unsubscribe; unsubscribed/bounced are terminal suppressed states no send can target.
  • Real analytics only. A metric with no data reads Not available yet — never a placeholder number.
  • Server-only secrets. The OpenAI key, the Resend key, and the Supabase service-role key never reach the browser; each provider SDK is imported in exactly one file.
  • No scraped/purchased outreach. The system refuses cold-list imports and flags suspicious ones; the agent will refuse a request to email a non-consented list or to use fake urgency.
Decisions for sign-off (defaults chosen — flag any to change)
  • Default 4-email sequence default — Welcome+Problem → Solution+Credibility → Objection-handling → Offer/Final-CTA. The creator can add/remove steps; the AI honors the chosen goal.
  • MVP sender = platform's verified Resend domain default. A sender_identity record exists so per-creator verified domains (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) swap in later with no flow change.
  • One active campaign per course for MVP default. The schema allows many; the UI assumes one to keep the model simple.
  • Editing an approved email returns the campaign to In Review default — future unsent steps re-require compliance + approval; already-sent emails are immutable.
  • Follow-up rules are approved with the campaign default; the agent may not add or change a live rule outside the approved set.

Borrow the creator-first list & deliverability hygiene; reject the blank-canvas copywriter.

No competitor screenshots were supplied, so this is reasoned from well-known product patterns and marked as inference. We compare only the decision-relevant dimensions for an email-campaign agent: where the copy comes from, how consent & the list work, how automation and follow-ups are built, how much autonomy the tool has, and what the creator must still do by hand.

Mailchimp

Net takeaways — where we win

1. Grounding is the moat. Every competitor starts from a blank canvas; we start from the finished course — subject lines, problem framings, objections, and CTAs are drawn from real outcomes and modules. 2. Reviewed autonomy is the differentiator. No creator email tool runs a plan→write→compliance-review→approve→auto-send-and-follow-up loop with a reversibility-graded brake. 3. Borrow the proven primitives — subscriber-as-state, consent-first forms, behavioral triggers, one-click unsubscribe, bounce/complaint suppression. 4. Reject the bloat — a goal and a sentence beat an automation-builder maze; the creator approves, not assembles.

Extend the three spines with a campaign lifecycle and a compliance gate on every send.

Approaches considered

Rejected: no course grounding, no reviewed-send loop, and the list + analytics live outside the product — fragmenting the very event stream the agent must observe.

B · Campaign layer on the existing spines ✓

Reuse the shipped tool layer + gate + event stream + scheduler; add campaign lifecycle, lead lists, compliance reviewer, follow-up rules, and the 26-tool chat.

Recommended: grounded, trustworthy, autonomous-capable, and mostly already built underneath — the new surface is the campaign model + copy intelligence + compliance, not new plumbing.

C · Standalone manual campaign builder

A form-driven sequence editor with manual copy only.

Deferred: no leverage and no assistant — and it's just the editor that already sits beneath B. We build it as B's substrate, not as the product.

Core product logic — the campaign lifecycle

A campaign is a state machine the creator drives and the agent assists. Generation and editing are reversible and free; the two irreversible boundaries — Approve and any send — require a human. Editing a step after approval drops the campaign back to In Review.

• Draft → Generated → In Review → Approved → Scheduled → Sending → Active → Completed
control / terminal: Paused Cancelled Failed

Approve and Sending are the only two gates. Everything before Approved is a reversible draft (generate/edit/regenerate/variants — auto-staged, rejectable). Pause halts future queued sends; Resume continues only the unsent ones; Cancel is terminal. Failed is entered only on unrecoverable provider/config errors and surfaces to the creator.

The governance gate — reversibility decides everything

Reused verbatim from the shipped foundation: one function every mutating tool call passes through, regardless of origin, routing on the tool's declared reversibility grade and recording to the marketing_action ledger. The PRD's “MarketingPatch” is a staged reversible action; “approve campaign / launch / send” are the irreversible ones.

Tool callReversibilityGate behaviorLedger status
read / analyticsn/a (no mutation)Executes immediately; never staged.not recorded
generate / edit / regenerate / variants / importReversibleExecutes immediately, captures a before_snapshot, stages the result as rejectable (a MarketingPatch). Reject replays the snapshot atomically.auto_approved → executed (or reverted)
approve / launch / send / activate / cancelIrreversibleDoes not execute. Logs a pending action, surfaces an approval request, pauses the agent loop. Human approve executes; deny discards.pending → approved/executed (or rejected)

Why one gate for every surface: a human clicking “Approve & send” in a card routes the same gate() as the agent calling launch_campaign. The autonomy boundary is the gate — the agent chains reversible steps freely but must stop at every irreversible one.

Two supporting state machines

Email send status (per lead × step)

Pending→Queued→Sent→Delivered→Opened→Clicked
BouncedFailedSkippedUnsubscribed

Materialized on the scheduled_send outbox; a unique (touch_id, subscriber_id) makes scheduler ticks idempotent. Skipped = suppressed at send time (unsub/bounce). Delivered/Opened/Clicked are advanced by provider events (webhooks in production; simulated in mock).

Lead / subscriber lifecycle (reducer over the event stream)

lead→subscribed→engaged→enrolled
unsubscribed · terminalbounced · terminal

Status is a reducer over analytics_event, not a column the app pokes. form_submit→lead, first email_sent→subscribed, email_open/click→engaged, enrollment→enrolled; email_unsubscribe/bounce→terminal suppressed. Consent is stamped at capture and required before a lead can be emailed.

The AI: one agent, seven separated responsibilities

Implemented as one workflow initially, with distinct roles the pipeline can route between — mirroring the studio's Content Agent phases. Copy roles run under a narrow generate tool-set; the orchestrator's sends always hit the gate.

◈ 1 · Course Marketing Analyst
Extracts audience, benefits, pain points, outcomes, objections, differentiators, credibility signals, and missing info from the course plan.
◇ 2 · Campaign Planner
Chooses the sequence structure, timing, follow-up logic, and CTA strategy for the selected goal. Output = the plan the creator approves before generation.
✎ 3 · Email Copywriter
Writes subject lines, preview text, body, CTA copy, and personalization variables for each step — grounded in the analyst's findings.
⎇ 4 · Variant Generator
Produces subject / CTA / hook / tone variants for the creator to pick. Selection only — never an A/B experiment.
⚖ 5 · Compliance & Trust Reviewer
Flags fake urgency, unsupported claims, spammy tone, misleading subjects, risky list source, missing unsubscribe, and aggressive frequency — before launch.
◴ 6 · Sending Orchestrator
Queues + drips sends on our scheduler, retries transient failures, suppresses unsub/bounce, handles pause/resume/cancel. All sends routed through the gate.
◉ 7 · Analytics Interpreter
Explains performance, weak emails, strong CTAs, drop-offs, and follow-up opportunities — without overclaiming causality from small data.

Key business rules

Approved autonomyThe AI generates and prepares freely; no send fires without a recorded human approval. Once approved, the orchestrator operates strictly inside the approved content, list, schedule, and follow-up rules.
Launch preconditions (one predicate)A campaign may launch only if: course has a title · a lead list with confirmed consent is attached · a sender identity is set · all steps are approved & compliance ≠ blocked · a schedule is chosen · every CTA URL resolves. Any failure blocks launch with a specific reason.
Consent & suppressionOnly consented leads are emailed. unsubscribed/bounced are terminal suppressed states; a global suppression list is checked at enqueue and at send. A click after unsubscribe is recorded but triggers no send.
Edit-after-approval → re-reviewEditing any future unsent step returns the campaign to In Review; that step re-requires compliance + approval. Already-sent emails are immutable.
Our scheduler, not the provider'sSequence + follow-up timing is our state machine on our scheduled_send outbox. Resend (or the mock) only moves bytes; it never owns sequence logic.
Idempotent sendsUnique (touch_id, subscriber_id) guarantees a subscriber never receives the same touch twice, even under repeated scheduler ticks or retries.
One event streamAll measurement is analytics_event. The dashboard, the observe step, and the lifecycle reducer read the same table — no metric computed two ways; missing data shows “Not available yet.”
Author scope by RLSCreators touch only their own courses' campaigns/lists/sequences via private.is_course_author(course_id). Public writes (leads, pageviews, unsubscribes) go through a service-role ingest route, never anon table writes.
Mock-first contractsTools depend on provider interfaces, not SDKs. The whole loop runs on a deterministic mock; Resend + cron swap in behind env-gated factories with zero contract changes.

The Marketing Chat tool surface (26 tools)

Every AI action is a tool call through the one executeMarketingTool(name, args, ctx) → gate() seam — the same contract behind the wizard, the cards, and the chat.   • Provider-agnostic EmailProvider interface (send / sendBatch, message-id tracking, webhooks, bounce/complaint, custom unsubscribe). Mock is deterministic; ResendEmailProvider is the only file importing the Resend SDK, selected when RESEND_API_KEY is set.
  • Sender identity — MVP sends from the platform's verified domain (RESEND_FROM); the sender_identity record is the seam for per-creator SPF/DKIM/DMARC verification later. A reply-to is always set.
  • Queue = the scheduled_send outbox. The tick route claims due pending rows, respects rate limits, retries transient failures, stops on pause, skips suppressed, and is idempotent via unique (touch_id, subscriber_id).
  • Unsubscribe & suppression — every marketing email carries a one-click unsubscribe + List-Unsubscribe header; opt-out marks the lead, suppresses future sends, and records the event. Transactional email (receipts) stays separate from marketing.

Stops are absolute: every rule halts on unsubscribe, bounce, or enroll. Rules are approved with the campaign; the agent may not add or mutate a live rule outside the approved set. Not in MVP: branching workflows, multi-channel follow-up, real-time behavioral automation beyond these triggers, reply classification.

Analytics taxonomy & the “real data only” rule

One append-only table, analytics_event, carries every measurable moment; the dashboard aggregates it, the reducer folds it into subscriber status, and getCampaignAnalytics serves it to the agent. Provider engagement events (delivered/open/click/bounce/complaint) arrive via Resend webhooks in production and are simulated deterministically in mock.

The rule: a metric with no underlying events renders Not available yet, never 0% dressed as data. Revenue/conversion metrics stay N/A until checkout data exists. The Analytics Interpreter must not overclaim causality from small samples.

Safety, compliance & the required footer

The AI cannot

  • Send to an unapproved or non-consented list, or add scraped/purchased contacts.
  • Ignore unsubscribe/suppression status, or send unapproved content.
  • Make guaranteed-outcome claims, use fake urgency, or invent false scarcity.
  • Hide marketing intent, or change a live campaign outside the approved rules.

Every marketing email includes

  • Sender / creator name and a clear reason the recipient is receiving it.
  • A working one-click unsubscribe (+ List-Unsubscribe header).
  • A mailing address / compliant business-identity field.
  • WiseSel attribution where required.

Pre-launch compliance checks (Screen 6): lead-source risk · consent status · missing unsubscribe · spammy language · misleading subjects · unsupported claims · aggressive frequency · broken CTA URLs · missing sender identity · missing approval. Blocking findings set compliance_status: "blocked" and disable launch.

05 — User Experience (Acceptance)

Self-acceptance stories — the core flows that must pass.

Each story is written from the actor's perspective and tied to concrete on-screen actions. This is core-flow acceptance, not exhaustive QA. Creator stories run in Marketing Studio; the lead story runs on the public page.

1Generate a full campaign from the course
User need

I finished my course; I want a complete, editable launch — not a blank email tab.

Flow

Open Marketing → Create campaign → pick goal “Launch this course” → choose a consented list → Generate.

Expected result

A 4-email sequence (Welcome+Problem → Solution → Objection → Offer) appears as staged drafts with subjects, previews, bodies, CTAs, timing, and follow-up rules — grounded in my syllabus. Nothing is sent.

2Edit an email via chat and accept the patch
User need

I want to revise copy by asking, and see exactly what changes before it sticks.

Flow

In the assistant, type “Make email 2 more concise and warmer” → review the before/after patch → Accept (or Reject).

Expected result

A MarketingPatch shows the affected email + any compliance warnings; Accept persists it; Reject restores the prior text exactly. The AI never edited state directly.

3Consent gates a lead list and a launch
User need

I want to add contacts safely and never email people who didn't opt in.

Flow

Open Leads → paste emails → try to skip the consent box (blocked) → check “I confirm…” → import → attach the list to the campaign.

Expected result

Import requires the consent confirmation; confirmed leads become eligible, unconfirmed/suppressed ones don't. A list without confirmed consent blocks launch with a clear reason.

4Approve, schedule, and let it send autonomously
User need

I want to email my list on a schedule — but I must be the one who says “go.”

Flow

Pass compliance → choose Drip · every 2 days → Approve & launch in the gate.

Expected result

Only after approval does sending begin; status advances to Sending → Active and the scheduler drips each email on cadence. Cancel in the gate leaves the list untouched. The agent paused until I decided.

5Unsubscribe is honored; analytics stay real
User need

(Lead) I want out in one click; (Creator) I want the numbers to be true.

Flow

A lead clicks unsubscribe in an email → the creator opens Analytics.

Expected result

The lead is marked unsubscribed, suppressed from all future sends, and future queued sends to them are cancelled. The dashboard shows real sent/open/click counts; metrics without data read “Not available yet.”

6Compliance catches a risky email before launch
User need

I don't want to send something spammy or over-promised by accident.

Flow

Ask the agent for “more urgency” with a fake deadline → run Compliance & Trust review.

Expected result

The reviewer refuses fake scarcity and flags it as blocking; Fix with AI rewrites it to truthful urgency. Launch stays disabled while a blocking finding exists.

Definition of done — Autonomous Email Marketing Agent v1

A creator opens Marketing Studio, picks a goal and a consented lead list, and gets a grounded 4-email campaign as staged, editable drafts; revises it by card or chat with every AI change staged as a rejectable MarketingPatch; passes a compliance review that blocks fake urgency / broken CTAs / non-consented lists; approves once and lets WiseSel send and follow up automatically on schedule — suppressing every unsubscribe and bounce — while one event stream drives real analytics and the assistant's next suggestion. All provider keys server-only, the whole loop running on mock providers, real Resend + cron swapping in by a single env var with no contract changes. Social/forum/offers are draft-only; A/B is variants-only; no scraped/purchased outreach; no send without a human's yes.
